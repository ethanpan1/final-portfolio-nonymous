Cheese Shop App

Creating an easy interface for online cheese shopping


Context: UI/UX Assignment

Task: Use React to build a list and aggregator interface.

Skills practiced: React, Javascript, designing for usability, clean code

Part 1: Tough Cheese

For this assignment, I created a cheese shop app called La Fromagerie, with a list interface serving as a display case for the cheeses and an aggregator serving as a shopping cart. The goal of the app is to help users know about the cheeses they're buying and provide an easy shopping experience.

My target audience is the consumer who is interested in entering the cheese world but does not know where to begin. Especially because of COVID, many food retailers have taken to the internet to sell their wares, but without the help of real-life cheesemongers, buyers can be lost in a sea of unfamiliar cheese names. Thus, the buying experience may be frustrating, intimidating, or confusing enough to stop them from purchasing at all.

Part 2: Get That Cheddar

My app serves as a model for online cheese shops by provide more cheese information up-front to the buyers. Filters and sorts help buyers find the cheese they're looking for, while additional descriptive info allows them to know what they're in for. My app also streamlines the buying process by placing the display case and the cart on the same page, much like a real cheese shop.
I considered a number of usability principles while designing the interface.
Principle Implementation
Interaction Filters, sorts, and buttons are clearly labelled. Correct interactions are made obvious (e.g., hover states).
Layout The cart is separated from the display case. Nice flow from filtering/sorting, to adding to cart, to checking out.
Text Information for each cheese is succinctly presented and displayed in both the display case and the cart.
Accessibility Color choices and font/image size are readable. Image alts are provided. Completely responsive except for mobile sizes.

Part 3: Cheesy Come, Cheesy Go

I also focused on the readability and extensibility of my code. I created a clear architecture with multiple components to keep my code flexible and reusable. I tried to strike a balance between factoring out common code and maintaining readability; for example, I used one Card class to handle CSS styling, but I created separate DisplayCard and CartCard components because they used different functions. I also made sure to write comments for every component and function.

This is the Github repository for the app, and this is the deployed website.

Major Takeaways

Consider what knowledge the user brings to the interface.
  • The specific usage of the filters and sorts came from understanding what a cheese buyer would need to know before making a purchase.
Usability comes from both functionality and visual design.
  • It is important to provide useful functionalities while also keeping other usability principles (layout, concision) in mind.
Code readability and efficiency can sometimes be at odds.
  • Reducing the number of lines in your code does not always mean it's easier to understand. Sometimes, two objects are better than one, even if they share similarities.
